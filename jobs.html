Full-Stack Web Developer

Reference Number: WD001

Salary: $75,000 – $95,000 per year

Location: Melbourne CBD (hybrid, 3 days in office)

Job Type: Full-time, permanent

Reporting Line: Reports to the Head of Digital

Short Description

We are seeking a Full-Stack Web Developer to build and maintain SolarCore's public-facing websites and customer portals. You will work across PHP, MySQL, HTML5, and CSS3 to deliver responsive, accessible, and secure web applications that help customers explore our renewable energy products and track their solar system performance.

Key Responsibilities

  1. Develop responsive web pages and dynamic features using PHP and MySQL.
  2. Design and maintain database schemas, write secure SQL queries with prepared statements.
  3. Build server-side form validation and authenticated administrator dashboards.
  4. Ensure WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility compliance across all customer-facing pages.
  5. Collaborate with UX designers and the marketing team to launch new campaigns.
  6. Maintain version control via Git and participate in peer code reviews.

Essential Requirements

  • Strong proficiency in PHP 7+ / 8+ and MySQL.
  • Solid HTML5 and CSS3 skills, including responsive design and Flexbox/Grid.
  • Understanding of web security: prepared statements, password hashing, session handling.
  • Experience with Git-based collaborative workflows.
  • Excellent written communication and team collaboration skills.

Desirable Requirements

  • Experience with the LAMP/XAMPP stack and Apache configuration.
  • Familiarity with Australian renewable energy industry or sustainability sector.
  • Knowledge of accessibility standards (WCAG) and SEO best practice.
  • Experience integrating REST APIs (e.g. solar inverter monitoring data).

Solar Systems Engineer

Reference Number: SE002

Salary: $85,000 – $110,000 per year

Location: Melbourne (with travel to project sites across Victoria)

Job Type: Full-time, permanent

Reporting Line: Reports to the Engineering Manager

Short Description

We are looking for a Solar Systems Engineer to design and oversee residential and commercial solar PV installations. You will be responsible for system sizing, electrical design, grid-connection paperwork, and on-site commissioning. This is a hands-on role for an engineer who wants their work to directly contribute to Australia's clean energy transition.

Key Responsibilities

  1. Design solar PV systems for residential, commercial, and small-utility clients.
  2. Perform site assessments, shading analysis, and energy yield modelling.
  3. Prepare single-line diagrams, electrical drawings, and Clean Energy Council compliance documentation.
  4. Liaise with distribution network service providers (DNSPs) for grid-connection approvals.
  5. Supervise installation crews and conduct final commissioning & safety testing.
  6. Mentor junior engineers and apprentice electricians.

Essential Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering or related discipline.
  • Clean Energy Council (CEC) accreditation for grid-connect solar PV design and install.
  • Minimum 3 years of experience in solar PV system design.
  • Proficiency with PVsyst, AutoCAD Electrical, and HelioScope (or similar).
  • Current Australian driver's licence and willingness to travel to regional sites.

Desirable Requirements

  • Experience with battery storage and hybrid system design.
  • Knowledge of AS/NZS 5033 and AS/NZS 4777 standards.
  • Project management certification (e.g. PRINCE2, AGILE).
  • Previous experience with utility-scale (> 100 kW) commercial installations.
